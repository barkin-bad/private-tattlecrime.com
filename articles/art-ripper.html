AN EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW

HOW THE RIPPER RIPS:

By Tattle Crime

Something terrible lurks within the walls of the Baltimore State Hospital for the Criminally Insane. Though many inmates at this Maximum Security "rehabilitation campus" boast some of the most horrific homicides on the Eastern Seaboard, one man has recently emerged as a different kind of killer. His name in Dr. Abel Gideon. And strong evidence has surfaced that he's far more than a mild-mannered surgeon who cruelly murdered his wife. Maybe, just maybe. Gideon is the most sought after serial killer at large - a killer who's eluded the FBI for years and has baffled their most "gifted" profilers. That serial killer? None other than the "Chesapeake Ripper." This would explain why the Ripper's been silent for more than two years.

That serial killer? None other than the "Chesapeake Ripper." This would explain why the Ripper's been silent for more than two years. So I did what any self-respecting purveyor of ghoulish truth would do. I went straight to the source. Through a patchwork of legal loopholes, a disgruntled orderly with a nicotine addiction, and one very expensive FOIA request, I obtained a supervised interview with Dr. Abel Gideon at the Baltimore State Hospital for the Criminally Insane. Maximum security. Glass partition.

Gideon didn't disappoint. He arrived in his orange jumpsuit, wrists cuffed to a leather belt, a thin sheen of something almost like amusement on his face. He looks less like a monster and more like your favorite high school biology teacher who just got caught embalming the class hamster. That's the terrifying part.

**Tattle Crime:** *Dr. Gideon, the FBI won't confirm your connection to the Chesapeake Ripper murders. But you haven't denied it either.* **Abel Gideon:** *(leans forward, chain links clinking)* “Denial is a social contract I never signed. The Ripper removes organs with surgical precision. I am a surgeon. The Ripper arranges bodies as tableaux. I've always believed surgery is theater - just with higher stakes and worse lighting.” **TC:** *The Ripper's victims were found with their lungs replaced by origami birds made of their own skin. That's not surgery. That's poetry. Violent, horrible poetry.* **Gideon:** *(smiles)* “All the best poetry is. You're Freddie Lounds. You write obituaries with adjectives. Don't pretend to be shocked by the ink.” He's got a point. I hate that he's got a point. **TC:** *Why stop killing for two years? Guilt? Fear? Ran out of Tupperware?* **Gideon:** “I didn't stop. I just changed my playlist. The Ripper you're looking for - the one who mailed a victim's kidney to the FBI with a recipe for fava bean puree - that wasn't about rage. That was about *conversation*. And conversations grow quiet when the other party stops listening.” I asked him who wasn't listening. He just tapped his temple twice with a manacled finger. **TC:** *Are you the Chesapeake Ripper?* **Gideon:** *(pause, then very softly)* “I am what happens when you tell a man he's a monster often enough that he starts to believe *monster* is a job title. Call me the Ripper if it helps you sleep at night. I know I will.” The orderly ended the session there. Gideon winked at me as they led him out. Not a romantic wink. A *knowing* wink. Like he'd just told me the punchline to a joke I haven't finished hearing yet. Back in my car, I found a small origami bird - made from a page of my own article - tucked under my windshield wiper. No note. No security camera saw who left it. Make of that what you will. But here's what I make of it: Abel Gideon may or may not be the Chesapeake Ripper. But he's *something*. And something has been very, very quiet for two years. Quiet things bite hardest.
