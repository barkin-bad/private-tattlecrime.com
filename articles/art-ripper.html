The Ripper's Secret Supper

by Tattle Crime ✦ october 27, 2025 ✦ 9 min read

Behind closed doors, deep within the FBI’s Behavioral Analysis Unit in Quantico, a select group of journalists, cold‑case analysts, and forensic psychologists gathered for what was cryptically labeled “The Ripper’s Secret Supper.” The invitation alone raised eyebrows: black‑bordered cards, no digital traces, and a strict off‑the‑record mandate. No recordings. No tweets. No follow‑up questions attributed to names.

For three hours, attendees were walked through a living nightmare that refuses to die — the 1888 Whitechapel murders attributed to Jack the Ripper. But this was no mere history lecture. The Bureau has been quietly cross‑referencing Ripper‑era evidence with unsolved modern serial killings across three continents. The hook? A signature rarely discussed in public: specific organ removal patterns that mirror post‑mortem mutilations found in five Interpol‑listed cases from 1985 to 2019.

“We’re not saying the same person, but someone is re‑ritualizing the Ripper playbook.” — FBI source, BAU

The dinner’s centerpiece was a single redacted file, codenamed “Leather Apron 2.0.” It contained geospatial profiling that overlays the original Ripper kill zones onto modern East London and, chillingly, three U.S. cities. An off‑the‑record source revealed that the FBI has been quietly advising Scotland Yard and the LAPD on a connected series of stabbings where the killer left Victorian‑era coins on the victims’ chests.

🕯️
Geospatial overlays matched Ripper’s original Whitechapel routes to modern metro systems — a signature of ritualistic staging, not random violence.

Attendees were sworn to secrecy not because of national security, but because the investigation is active — and fragile. One journalist asked the obvious: “If this is all off the record, why invite us at all?” The reply came with a thin smile: “Because one day soon, we’ll need you to understand how old evil learns new tricks. When we call, remember the supper.”

We will. Off the record or not, some stories refuse to stay buried. As the evening ended, each guest received a small card with only three words: “remember the supper.” No signature, no letterhead — just ink on blackstock. Whatever the FBI is tracking, they believe the pattern is accelerating. And when the silence breaks, tattlecrime will be there, whether they like it or not.
