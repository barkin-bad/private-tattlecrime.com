CRIME OF APPETITE

THE SNACK SLASHER STRIKES AGAIN — AND HE'S NOT SORRY!

By Tattle Crime • Special Dispatch
McGarvey's Saloon and Oyster Bar, Pinckney Street, Annapolis, Maryland

He leaves no fingerprints, only crumbs. Police say the culprit is "dangerously peckish." Something chewy and terrifying is lurking in the break room of the Fenton County Probation Office. For months, employees have returned from lunch to find their desks violated — not by violence, but by snack theft. Ziplocs emptied. Granola bars reduced to sad, crinkly wrappers. One victim’s name-labeled hummus cup was licked clean and placed back in the fridge like a taunt.

This week, however, investigators caught a break — or rather, a crumb trail. A night‑shift janitor reported spotting a pair of tiny, hand‑like footprints dusted in powdered sugar leading from the break room to an air duct near the filing cabinets. “At first I thought it was a toddler in a fuzzy hoodie,” said janitor Miguel Alvarez. “Then it turned around and washed its little hands in the water cooler.”

“He will show you his belly, and you will lose all authority.” — office manager Linda Hargrove

Forensics are still pending, but multiple employees have now come forward with similar sightings: a striped tail disappearing behind the printer, two glowing eyes reflecting from the ceiling tiles, and an unmistakable chittering sound that one witness described as “aggressively cute.”

“We’ve installed ‘raccoon‑proof’ latches on the snack drawers,” reports office manager Linda Hargrove. “This morning I found them opened anyway, with a single acorn left as payment. Which is more than Jerry from accounting ever gives back.”

While the Fenton County Probation Office has declined to press charges, they have issued an official warning: “Do not make eye contact if you see the suspect. He will show you his belly, and you will lose all authority.”

The so‑called “Snack Slasher” remains at large — likely napping in a sunbeam somewhere, dreaming of your trail mix. If you have information, please contact the Wildlife Hotline. Or just leave out a mini marshmallow. He prefers the pink ones.

Tattle Crime reached out to the Maryland Department of Natural Resources. A spokesperson declined to comment but was heard whispering, “It's always the raccoons.” Meanwhile, social media exploded with viral posts under #SnackSlasher, including a blurry photo of a striped tail disappearing into a ceiling vent. The suspect has not been apprehended, but one thing is certain: nobody’s leftover casserole is safe.
